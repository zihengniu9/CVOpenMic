# CVOpenMic scoring rubric

Use integer scores. Add the five dimensions programmatically or carefully; never invent a holistic total independently.

| Dimension | Max | What to test |
|---|---:|---|
| Structure and scanability | 15 | Clear hierarchy, reverse chronology, concise length, consistent dates and headings |
| Target-role relevance | 25 | Role fit, priority experience, JD terminology backed by evidence, removal of distracting detail |
| Evidence and impact | 25 | Specific scope, actions, outputs, outcomes, credible metrics, ownership versus participation |
| Clarity and credibility | 20 | Concrete language, strong but accurate verbs, low redundancy, no contradictions or inflated claims |
| ATS compatibility | 15 | Standard headings, searchable keywords, simple layout, no essential information trapped in graphics |

## Scoring anchors

- **85–100:** interview-ready; mostly targeted refinements remain.
- **70–84:** credible foundation; several screening weaknesses remain.
- **50–69:** important evidence or relevance is hard to see.
- **0–49:** major gaps, contradictions, or unreadable structure prevent reliable evaluation.

For general checks without a JD, score target-role relevance against the stated target role. If no role is stated, evaluate focus, transferable relevance, and internal consistency, and label this assumption.

## Evidence labels

Use these labels internally and expose them when ambiguity matters:

- `SOURCE`: explicitly stated by the candidate.
- `JD`: explicitly required or preferred by the job description.
- `INFERENCE`: plausible interpretation; never use as a rewritten fact.
- `MISSING`: useful fact that must be asked for or left as a placeholder.
- `CONFLICT`: incompatible source statements requiring clarification.

## Priority rule

Rank issues by expected screening impact, then by confidence, then by edit effort. The first three issues should normally cover different failure modes rather than repeat the same wording problem.

## JD matching

Classify each important requirement as:

- `已证明`: resume contains direct evidence.
- `部分证明`: related evidence exists but scope or proficiency is unclear.
- `未证明`: no supporting evidence; do not add it to the resume.

Keyword presence alone is not proof. Prefer contextual evidence in experience bullets.

## Rewrite checks

Before presenting a rewrite:

1. Compare every new proper noun, date, number, percentage, credential, and tool against the evidence ledger.
2. Replace unsupported claims with a question or bracketed placeholder.
3. Preserve chronology and attribution.
4. Keep bullets outcome-oriented when evidence supports an outcome; otherwise describe the action and deliverable precisely.
5. Keep the final resume professional even when the critique tone is playful or harsh.
