# Complete CV architecture

Use this reference whenever producing a full CV, restructuring sections, or deciding what can be compressed. A targeted CV is still a complete professional history, not a collection of selected highlights.

## Structural contract

When the source contains them, retain these anchors:

1. `Header` — name, target role, supplied contact details, and supplied portfolio links.
2. `Professional summary` — optional but useful for mixed backgrounds or a career pivot; lead with target, differentiator, and strongest proof.
3. `Education` — retain every degree, active or completed program, minor, and joint program that the source treats as education.
4. `Work experience` — retain every employer and internship entry. Compress low-relevance roles; do not silently erase them.
5. `Projects` — retain representative projects that prove target-role skills, ownership, or measurable outcomes.
6. `Research and publications` — retain when academically meaningful or relevant to research, data, strategy, or technical roles; compress secondary publications before deleting first-author work.
7. `Awards` — select the strongest, most credible awards; combine minor awards into one line when space is tight.
8. `Skills` — group by function and evidence. Avoid self-rated proficiency bars and generic personality claims.

Do not promote a project to work experience, a joint program to an independent degree, coursework to employment, or tool exposure to product ownership.

## Order by candidate type

### New graduate or early career

`Header → Target summary → Education → Work experience → Target-role projects → Research/publications → Awards → Skills`

Education stays near the top because degree status and academic foundation are active screening signals. Work experience must still be a distinct section, even when every role is an internship.

### Experienced individual contributor or manager

`Header → Target summary → Work experience → Selected projects or leadership → Education → Skills/certifications`

### Research or academic candidate

`Header → Research summary → Education → Publications → Research experience → Teaching/work experience → Awards → Skills`

### AI strategy or AI product, early career

`Header → AI strategy summary → Education → Work experience → AI/strategy projects → Research/publications → Awards and skills`

This order preserves chronology and credibility while making the target narrative visible. Do not move all formal history behind an AI project simply because AI is the target.

## Entry schemas

### Education

Use one entry per institution or clearly defined program:

`Institution | Degree and field | Dates`

Optional second line: minor, joint-program status, honors, thesis, or highly relevant coursework. State `joint program`, `exchange`, or `dual degree` exactly as supported; do not collapse distinct institutions into one ambiguous line.

### Work experience

Use one entry per employer:

`Employer | Department or team | Role | Dates`

Follow with 1–4 bullets. Preserve the role even when only one concise bullet is relevant. Keep responsibilities, outputs, and results attributable to the candidate.

### Project experience

Use:

`Project | Candidate role | Dates`

Then show the user or business problem, the candidate's decision or action, the method, and supported results. Label personal, course, competition, and commercial projects accurately.

### Publications

Use a compact, consistent citation containing supported author position, title, venue, year, and optional DOI or link. Separate first-author work from participation when that distinction matters.

## Conclusion and data first

Within each section, order entries by relevance and reverse chronology unless a stronger screening reason exists. Within each entry, lead each bullet with the strongest supported conclusion.

Preferred hierarchy:

1. **Outcome** — adoption, growth, revenue or profit, cost, quality, time, decision impact.
2. **Scale or deliverable** — users, records, projects, reports, models, content, launches.
3. **Action and method** — what the candidate did and how.
4. **Context** — background only when needed to understand the action.

Useful forms:

- `[结果或数据]｜通过[真实动作与方法]完成[真实范围]，带来[业务意义]。`
- `[明确结论或交付物]｜负责[真实动作]，使用[真实方法]解决[真实问题]。`
- `[规模]｜在[真实场景]承担[真实职责]，完成[真实输出]。`

Examples must remain placeholders. Never copy example metrics into a candidate's CV.

Do not force a metric to the front when it creates awkward language or weakens causality. If no outcome exists, lead with a precise deliverable or decision. `Completed an investor-research brief` is stronger than a fabricated percentage and clearer than `Responsible for research`.

## Compression ladder

When the CV is too long, compress in this order:

1. remove repeated adjectives, self-evaluation, and generic duties;
2. merge overlapping bullets within the same entry;
3. shorten low-relevance entries to one evidence-bearing bullet;
4. combine minor awards or tools into compact lines;
5. remove low-value project detail;
6. remove an entire education or work entry only with explicit user approval.

Two coherent pages are better than one page that erases chronology or proof.

## Page count and visual density

Choose page count after the final copy is known. Never preserve the source page count by default and never insert a hard page break only to keep modules visually separate.

- Prefer one page when all required content remains readable with approximately 9.5–11 pt body text, 12–18 mm margins, clear hierarchy, and normal line spacing.
- Use two pages when one page would require tiny type, cramped spacing, missing entries, or an ATS-hostile layout.
- For a two-page CV, aim for roughly 70–90% body-area utilization on each page. The last page should normally use at least 60% of its available body height.
- Treat an empty lower half as a layout failure. If any page leaves more than about 35% of usable body height empty, reconsider the page count or rebalance section placement before delivery.
- Avoid forced page breaks between `Work experience`, `Projects`, and `Research`. Allow the next section to start on the current page when its heading and at least one complete entry fit.
- Keep an individual education, work, or project entry together when possible; do not keep an entire long section together if that creates a blank page region.
- Rebalance in this order: remove unnecessary hard breaks, move complete entries across pages, adjust section spacing, adjust body text within the readable range, then reconsider one versus two pages.
- Do not fill space with invented claims, repeated summaries, oversized decoration, excessive metric cards, or unrelated Bazi content.

For rendered images, PDF, or DOCX, inspect every page at normal viewing size. Confirm there is no clipped text, orphan heading, punctuation stranded at the start of a line, visibly unbalanced page, or large empty lower region.

## Editable delivery workflow

Use DOCX as the canonical final artifact for a completed CV unless the user explicitly requests another editable format.

1. Generate the complete CV as an editable Word document using native paragraphs, styles, real lists, and simple tables only where the information is genuinely tabular.
2. Render the DOCX to PDF from the same file. Do not independently recreate the PDF or image layout.
3. Inspect every rendered PDF page as an image and revise the DOCX until content, pagination, visual density, fonts, and spacing pass QA.
4. Deliver the DOCX for editing and the PDF for preview or application submission when the user requests both.
5. For later corrections, edit the DOCX first and regenerate all previews from it. Never patch only the PDF or image.

Do not flatten the resume into a screenshot as the primary deliverable. Images are previews, not the source of truth.

## Architecture checklist

Before delivery, verify:

- every source institution and degree appears with accurate status;
- every source employer and role appears, unless omission was explicitly approved;
- education, work, and projects remain distinct sections;
- dates use one consistent format and reverse chronology;
- the first third of the CV states the target and strongest supported proof;
- strong data or conclusions appear before process detail;
- first-author work, ownership, and commercial results are not buried;
- no Bazi-derived statement appears in the CV;
- compressed entries remain factually recognizable rather than disappearing.
- page count follows content volume rather than the source file or a fixed template;
- no page has a large unused lower half when the content could be reflowed legibly;
- visual density is improved by reflow and typography, never by invented or repetitive content.
- the delivered PDF was rendered from the delivered DOCX and contains the same copy;
- the DOCX remains editable: no full-page resume screenshot, flattened text, or image-only sections.
